Phytomorphology

Cacti are highly specialized, xeromorphic plants with succulent stems adapted for water storage and photosynthesis, typically lacking functional leaves. Key features include protective spine-bearing areoles (modified buds), thick waxy cuticles, and extensive, shallow root systems for rapid water absorption, often employing Crassulacean Acid Metabolism (CAM) to minimize water loss.
